Frequently Asked Questions

Find answers about joining Tatle tale, workshops, materials, privacy, and community care.

Studio & Community Care

Do I need to bring any materials? +

Just yourself! We provide everything—from the high-quality materials to the professional tools.

What if I need to cancel my booking? +

We understand that life happens. If you can’t make it, please just send us an email or give us a call so we can offer your spot to someone else in the community.

How do you handle photography and privacy? +

We love sharing our community’s progress, but we respect your privacy above all else. We will always ask you to sign a consent form before taking any photos for the website or social media.

Is the venue wheelchair accessible? +

Unfortunately, we are not currently able to offer full wheelchair accessibility at our venue.

We are aware that this is a limitation and we are genuinely sorry for any inconvenience this causes. We do not yet have the infrastructure in place to ensure a fully accessible experience.

If you have specific access needs or questions about what we can accommodate, please contact us before attending and we will do our best to help.

Improving accessibility is something we are actively working towards for future workshops and events.

These workshops are for everyone 18 and above — no experience, no materials, no pressure. Just bring yourself.

Joining the Circle

Is there a cost to attend? +

No. Tatle tale is completely free for all community members, including new faces! We want to ensure that creativity and connection are accessible to everyone in Sheffield.

Where and when do the workshops take place? +

We update everything on our website and Instagram account - tatle.tale.

Is there a minimum age to join the workshops? +

Yes — our workshops are open to participants aged 18 and over.

There is no upper age limit whatsoever. Tatle tale is built on the belief that creativity and connection have no expiry date, and we warmly welcome participants of all ages above 18 — whether you are 20 or 90.

The intergenerational nature of these workshops is central to everything we do, and we hope to see as wide a range of ages around the table as possible.

How do I secure my spot? +

You can book your place directly through this website, or if you're passing by, feel free to just show up! We always keep a few spots open for spontaneous creators.
