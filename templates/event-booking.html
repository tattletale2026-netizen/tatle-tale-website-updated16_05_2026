The Story Behind These Workshops

Where old hands meet new eyes

Every piece of cloth carries a memory. A curtain from a childhood home. A dress worn on a first day. A jacket that kept someone warm for twenty winters. Most of these things end up in landfill — not because they've lost their meaning, but because we've forgotten to look at them slowly enough.

Belinda Simcox has spent years doing exactly that — looking slowly. Through her work, she has transformed ordinary, discarded and donated fabrics into handmade soft toys and memory bears, each one carrying the quiet story of its material.

Tatle tale is a Sheffield-based intergenerational art project that believes the most powerful creative exchange happens in the unhurried space between generations — where a memory surfaces, a skill is passed on, and something new is made together.

These workshops are our first collaboration. They are designed for adults above 18+, irrespective of background, or creative experience. You don't need to know how to sew. You just need to be willing to sit, listen, and make.

“This is not about making something perfect. It's about making something together.”

Workshop Facilitator

Belinda Simcox

Belinda's Soft Toys & Memory Bears — Moor Market, Sheffield

Belinda Simcox is a master of the needle and a guardian of memories. Based in the heart of Sheffield's Moor Market, she has dedicated her craft to breathing new life into forgotten fabrics. From bespoke memory bears that hold the essence of loved ones to whimsical soft toys that spark joy, Belinda's work is a testament to the power of handmade craft.

Through Tatle tale, Belinda shares not just her technical skills, but her philosophy of slow, meaningful making. Her workshops are spaces of warmth, where the act of stitching becomes a bridge between people and their stories.

Where Tatle tale Meets an Owl

The soft toy & plushies sessions — making something that matters

Age: 18+ only
“Everything is provided — just bring yourself. And if you have an old piece of fabric you love too much to throw away, bring that too. Belinda will help you turn it into your best friend.”

Over four intensive and joyful sessions, participants will dive deep into the craft of toy making. Under Belinda's expert guidance, you'll learn the techniques needed to transform your chosen materials into a unique companion.

Mutual learning
Creative experimentation
Upcycling & Sustainability
Traditional Craft

Session Structure

0–5 mins Arrival & reconnection
5–35 mins Making time
35–45 mins Show, tell & reflect
Break 10 minute rest

Across the four sessions, participants will:

  • Learn fundamental hand-stitching and construction techniques.
  • Understand the properties of different recycled fabrics.
  • Follow (and adapt) professional soft toy patterns.
  • Master the art of stuffing and finishing for durability and character.
  • Contribute to a collective record of stories and skills.
